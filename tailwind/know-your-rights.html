How the Capsule Pyrale Works

The Three-Step Process

From the moment the Capsule Pyrale is received, every gesture is deliberate, respectful, and guided by years of research. The process is designed to honor the body and offer peace of mind to the family — from the first step to the final seal.

Step 1 — Gentle Placement

Respectful Handling

The body is placed inside the Capsule Pyrale by qualified professionals using a discreet, secure system. The dual hydraulic mechanism — assisted opening and closing — allows for smooth, controlled movement. Every gesture is slow and deliberate. There is no rush. The process is designed to reflect the care the person deserved in life.

Interior

Integrated LED lighting (customizable color) creates a serene, dignified environment inside the capsule from the moment of placement.

Mechanism

Dual hydraulic system ensures the cover opens and closes smoothly without effort, eliminating any abruptness from the process.

Step 2 — Hermetic Sealing

Airtight & Odorless

The polycarbonate cover — aviation-grade, unbreakable — is lowered and locked using a specialized hermetic sealing key. The high-density industrial rubber joints create a completely airtight barrier. No leaks. No odors. No external infiltration of any kind. The seal is permanent and verifiable.

Cover Material

Ultra-resistant polycarbonate, the same material used in aviation and industrial applications. Transparent, unbreakable, and scratch-resistant.

Sealing System

Hermetic key mechanism with high-density rubber joints ensures zero air leakage. The integrity of the seal is confirmed before the capsule leaves the care of the professional.

Step 3 — Inert Gas Preservation

Chemical-Free · 60–100 Years

Once sealed, the capsule is filled with inert nitrogen gas. Nitrogen — an element that makes up 78% of the air we breathe — naturally displaces oxygen inside the capsule. Without oxygen, the biological processes that cause decomposition cannot occur. The body dries gently and naturally, preserved without any chemicals, embalming agents, or toxic byproducts. Estimated preservation: 60 to 100 years.

The Gas

Pure inert nitrogen. Non-toxic, non-reactive, and entirely natural. No embalming chemicals, no formaldehyde, no toxic release — ever.

Preservation Period

Estimated 60 to 100 years of preservation — giving families the time they need to gather, grieve together, and say goodbye without urgency.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does nitrogen gas preserve the body? + −
Decomposition is driven primarily by oxygen and the microorganisms that thrive in its presence. By filling the sealed capsule with inert nitrogen gas, all oxygen is displaced. In this anaerobic (oxygen-free) environment, the biological processes responsible for decomposition cannot take hold. The body dries slowly and naturally, remaining stable for an estimated 60 to 100 years. No chemicals are introduced at any point.
Is the Capsule Pyrale environmentally safe? + −
Yes. The Capsule Pyrale was designed with environmental responsibility as a core principle. The main structure is made of 100% recyclable aluminum. The inert nitrogen gas is non-toxic and does not react with surrounding soil or water. There are no embalming chemicals, no formaldehyde, and no toxic leachate of any kind. Unlike traditional burial, the Capsule Pyrale has no impact on groundwater.
Can the Capsule Pyrale be transported internationally? + −
Yes. The hermetic seal produces zero odor and zero fluid leakage, which makes the Capsule Pyrale compatible with air cargo regulations for international repatriation. Families dealing with a death abroad — or wishing to transport a loved one across borders — will find the Capsule Pyrale far simpler to handle than traditional alternatives. Contact us to discuss your specific repatriation needs.
What is the preservation period? + −
Based on over a decade of research (first test: 2011; conclusive results: 2014), the Capsule Pyrale is estimated to preserve the body for 60 to 100 years. This extended period allows families to take the time they need — to gather from across the country or the world, to grieve without rush, and to say goodbye on their own terms.
Is it compatible with religious and cultural traditions? + −
The Capsule Pyrale is designed with cultural openness as a foundational value. Because it involves no chemicals, no disfigurement, and preserves the body in a natural state, it is compatible with many religious traditions that emphasize the dignity of the body. We encourage families to discuss their specific traditions with their religious or cultural leader. Our team is always available to address particular concerns with sensitivity and care.
Can we still have a viewing? + −
Yes — and for many families, this is one of the most meaningful aspects of the Capsule Pyrale. The ultra-resistant polycarbonate cover is transparent, allowing family members to be present with their loved one in a serene, dignified setting. There are no odors. The integrated LED lighting can be customized to create the atmosphere the family wishes. Extended viewing — across days or even weeks — becomes possible.
What happens after 60–100 years? + −
After the preservation period, the body will naturally begin to decompose as it would in a standard burial environment — but with no toxic trace from chemicals or embalming agents. The recyclable aluminum structure can then be repurposed. The Capsule Pyrale is designed to leave no lasting negative impact on the earth.
Is embalming still required? + −
No. The Capsule Pyrale is specifically designed to replace embalming. The hermetic nitrogen environment achieves natural preservation without any chemical intervention. This is one of the product's most important distinctions — for families who care about the environment, about what enters the body, and about a farewell that is genuinely clean and dignified.
