Born from a childhood promise. Built for every family.

As a boy, Pierre Béliveau lost a childhood friend to drowning. At the funeral, he was struck by a thought that never left him: that burial also meant contact with rising groundwater. That memory became a lifelong mission — to find a gentler, more dignified way to honor the departed.

After years of research, Pierre created the Capsule Pyrale. First test: 2011. Conclusive results: 2014. More than a technical achievement — a tribute rooted in dignity, peace, and the belief that the last gesture we offer should reflect the true worth of the ones we love.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does nitrogen gas preserve the body? ›
Once the Capsule Pyrale is hermetically sealed, it is filled with inert nitrogen gas. Nitrogen displaces oxygen inside the capsule, eliminating the conditions that allow decomposition to occur. The gas naturally dries the body over time — a process that is entirely physical, with no chemical agents involved. The result is preservation of up to 60 to 100 years.
Is the Capsule Pyrale environmentally safe? ›
Yes. The Capsule Pyrale uses no chemicals, embalming fluids, or toxic substances. The capsule itself is made from 100% recyclable aluminum and aviation-grade polycarbonate. There is no soil contamination, no groundwater impact, and no harmful release. It is one of the most ecologically responsible options currently available for final disposition.
Can the Capsule Pyrale be transported internationally? ›
Yes. Because the capsule is hermetically sealed and contains no fluid or odor, it is fully compliant with international air cargo regulations for the transport of human remains. It is certified in Canada and the USA, and additional country certifications are in progress. For families requiring repatriation, the Capsule Pyrale eliminates many of the logistical barriers associated with traditional preservation.
Is the Capsule Pyrale compatible with religious traditions? ›
The Capsule Pyrale has been used by families of diverse religious and cultural backgrounds. It is compatible with viewing rites, funeral ceremonies, and many interment traditions. We approach every tradition with sensitivity and openness. We encourage families to consult with their religious or community leaders, and our team is always available to discuss how the capsule can be integrated respectfully into any ceremony.
