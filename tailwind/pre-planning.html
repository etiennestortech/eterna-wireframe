Planning Ahead: 4 Meaningful Steps

01Have the Conversation + −
Start by sharing your wishes with the people closest to you. You don't need all the answers right away — simply opening the conversation is the most important step. Talking about it in a calm, unhurried moment removes the need for your loved ones to guess under pressure. We can guide you through it.
02Document Your Values and Wishes + −
Record your preferences — the type of farewell you envision, your feelings about eco-conscious choices, whether a Capsule Pyrale aligns with your values, and who you'd want to be present. Written documentation removes ambiguity and protects the people you love from having to make painful judgment calls during their most difficult days.
03Contact Us to Discuss Your Options + −
Reach out to our team at your own pace. We'll walk you through the Capsule Pyrale — its design, its technology, its customization options (color, LED, engraving) — and connect you with a funeral home partner near you. There is no obligation. Every conversation is handled with care and discretion. When you're ready, we can prepare a quote tailored to your situation.
04Share the Plan With Your Family + −
Let your family know where your documents are stored and who to contact. We recommend keeping a copy with your will and sharing it with a trusted person close to you. When the time comes, your family will have everything they need — and they will be free to grieve rather than scramble.

Common Concerns, Honestly Addressed

We hear these every day. Here's what we've learned.

Concern

"It feels morbid to plan for my own death."

A Different Way to See It

Planning ahead is one of the most loving things you can do for your family. It removes an impossible set of decisions from people who will be grieving, and ensures your farewell truly reflects your values. Most people feel a profound sense of relief — not dread — after completing it.

Concern

"I'm not old or sick. This isn't relevant to me yet."

A Different Way to See It

Age has nothing to do with it. The families who find themselves most overwhelmed are those with no plan in place — regardless of age. Planning now means the people you love won't face impossible choices in the hardest hours of their lives.

Concern

"What if my wishes change later?"

A Different Way to See It

Plans can always evolve. A pre-planning document is a living record, not a binding contract. What matters is having something on record — even an imperfect plan is infinitely better than none at all when the moment arrives.
